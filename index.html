The NIS 2 Directive Challenge

Strict Reporting Timelines

NIS 2 imposes a multi-stage reporting process with tight deadlines, demanding rapid detection and assessment.

24h

Initial "early warning" notification.

72h

Detailed follow-up and impact assessment.

1m

Comprehensive final report.

Significant Penalties

Non-compliance carries substantial financial risks for essential entities.

Corporate Accountability

The directive places direct responsibility on senior management for overseeing and implementing cybersecurity measures, with potential personal liability for non-compliance.

IaaS Shared Responsibility Model

Dividing Security Duties

In an IaaS model, security is a partnership. The cloud provider secures the underlying infrastructure, while the customer is responsible for everything they build upon it. This distinction is critical for defining the scope of incident response.

Foundation: Zero Trust Principles

Never Trust, Always Verify

Zero Trust acts as a proactive containment strategy. By assuming a breach is always possible, it enforces strict access controls, micro-segmentation, and continuous verification. This limits an attacker's ability to move laterally, significantly reducing the potential blast radius of an incident before it's even detected.
